\abstract{Whole-body magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) is established in pediatric oncology because it surveys the body without the ionizing radiation of computed tomography.
Automated analysis of these examinations depends on reliable anatomical reference, yet public segmentation datasets remain overwhelmingly adult, and models built on them transfer poorly to children whose anatomy changes across development.
We present a dataset of several hundred pediatric whole-body MRI examinations from a single-center lymphoma cohort, of which approximately 40 carry manual multi-structure normal-anatomy segmentations.
The segmentations span major skeletal structures and abdominal and thoracic organs across the pediatric age range, and were manually contoured.
We describe the cohort, the image acquisition, the de-identification and annotation procedures, the label organization, and the technical validation of label quality.
The dataset provides a normal-anatomy segmentation reference for pediatric whole-body MRI, intended for anatomical segmentation model development, adult-to-pediatric transfer learning, and downstream lesion-detection and staging research on the same imaging.}
