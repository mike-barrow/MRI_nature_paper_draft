\abstract{Whole-body magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) is established in pediatric oncology because it surveys the body without the ionizing radiation of computed tomography.
Automated analysis of these examinations depends on reliable anatomical reference, yet public segmentation datasets remain overwhelmingly adult, and models built on them transfer poorly to children whose anatomy changes across development.
We present a dataset of approximately [TBD] pediatric whole-body MRI examinations from a single-center lymphoma cohort, with a subset of approximately [TBD] examinations carrying manual multi-structure normal-anatomy segmentations.
The segmentations span major skeletal structures and abdominal and thoracic organs, and were contoured in 3D Slicer.
We describe the cohort, the image acquisition, the de-identification and annotation procedures, the file organization, and the technical validation of label quality.
The dataset is intended for pediatric anatomical segmentation model development, adult-to-pediatric transfer learning, and as a normal-anatomy reference for downstream lesion-detection and staging research on the same imaging.}
