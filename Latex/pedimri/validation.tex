\section*{Technical Validation}\label{sec:validation}

The analyses in this section assess whether the released segmentation labels are of sufficient quality and consistency for the intended reuse, and whether the data support the development of a usable pediatric whole-body anatomy-segmentation model.
They are reported as evidence of fitness for purpose and not as scientific findings.

\subsection*{Label-quality and image-quality screening}\label{subsec:qc}
Each labeled examination was screened by an experienced reader to confirm that the imaging is of diagnostic quality and that the segmentation covers the intended structures without gross error.
Because the segmentations are produced and corrected by trained readers, this screen functions chiefly to exclude examinations whose image quality is too low to support reliable contouring rather than to grade otherwise usable labels.
Examinations that failed the screen were excluded, and the counts are reported in the Participants subsection (Section~\ref{sec:methods}).

\subsection*{Inter-reader agreement}\label{subsec:agreement}
To quantify the reliability of the manual labels, a subset of examinations was segmented independently by more than one reader, and agreement between readers was measured.
Per-structure overlap between readers was measured with the Dice similarity coefficient, and a chance-corrected agreement statistic was computed to account for agreement expected by chance [TBD: state the statistic used, for example Gwet AC2 or Krippendorff alpha, and add its methods citation].
Agreement is reported per structure with 95\% confidence intervals, and never as a bare mean.
Figure~\ref{fig:agreement} shows the per-structure agreement, and Table~\ref{tab:metrics} reports the values.

\subsection*{Qualitative rating}\label{subsec:likert}
In addition to the overlap analysis, readers rated label quality on a five-point ordinal scale, with at least two readers per examination, so that label adequacy is captured by a direct expert judgment alongside the geometric overlap measures.
The rating distribution is reported per structure [TBD: report the rating distribution and the number of readers].

\subsection*{Fit-for-purpose segmentation baseline}\label{subsec:baseline}
To demonstrate that the released labels are sufficient to train a usable pediatric whole-body MRI anatomy-segmentation model, a baseline model was trained on the segmentation subset using an established self-configuring segmentation framework~\cite{Isensee_2021} and evaluated on a held-out portion of the subset.
Segmentation quality was measured with the Dice similarity coefficient and the 95th-percentile Hausdorff distance (HD95), reported per structure with 95\% confidence intervals obtained by bootstrap resampling.
For context, the same held-out examinations were also segmented with an adult-trained whole-body MRI tool~\cite{Wasserthal_2025} applied without further training, which provides a reference point for the difficulty of the pediatric anatomy and is reported only to characterize the data, not as a comparative result.
The values are reported in Table~\ref{tab:metrics} and illustrated qualitatively in Figure~\ref{fig:baseline}.
These figures are drawn from the separate study that developed the model on this dataset~\cite{[TBD: companion findings publication, cite once available]}, and they are presented here as evidence that the data are fit for the intended segmentation task.

\subsection*{Metadata and data integrity}\label{subsec:integrity}
The integrity and internal consistency of the release were verified by schema validation of the metadata table, checks of referential consistency between the metadata and the imaging and label files, and per-file checksums.
