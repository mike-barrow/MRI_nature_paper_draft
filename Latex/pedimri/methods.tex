\subsection*{Ethics statement}\label{subsec:ethics}

The released imaging is multi-protocol in origin, and the per-case protocol provenance is recorded in the accompanying metadata [TBD: confirm the provenance map].

De-identification follows a HIPAA Expert Determination standard rather than Safe Harbor alone, because whole-body imaging of minors can carry residual identifiability that Safe Harbor does not fully address [TBD: confirm the Expert Determination provider and certification].
Protected health information is removed from the DICOM headers, and a per-subject date offset is applied so that the relative timing between a subject's examinations is preserved.
Because whole-body MRI can permit surface reconstruction, identifiable regions are obscured.
The face is defaced using a published volumetric defacing workflow~\cite{Selfridge_2023}, and the pelvic region is similarly obscured [TBD: pelvic defacing tool and parameters].
The dataset is shared in a manner consistent with the FAIR principles for data reuse~\cite{Wilkinson_2016}.

\subsection*{Annotation}\label{subsec:annotation}
Manual segmentation was performed in 3D Slicer [TBD: add the 3D Slicer tool citation if a tool reference is required by the section].
Annotation was carried out by a team of readers of varying experience, comprising two postdoctoral physicians in pediatric radiology research and two medical or doctoral students.
The released labels cover 13 anatomical structures.
Seven are bilateral and stored as separate left and right instances, namely the femur, tibia, fibula, humerus, hip, lung, and kidney.
The remaining six are single structures, namely the spine, sacrum, liver, spleen, heart, and urinary bladder.
The seven bilateral structures contribute two labels each and the six single structures one label each, for 20 labels in total.
The radius, ulna, pancreas, great vessels, and thymus were outside the released label scope.
The full mapping between label indices and structures is provided as a data dictionary with the release (Section~\ref{sec:datarecords}) [TBD: confirm indices against the deposited files].

Two annotation protocols were used, and the released metadata records which protocol applies to each labeled examination.
For the training portion of the subset, labels were produced by correction, starting from automated predictions of an adult-trained whole-body MRI segmentation tool~\cite{Wasserthal_2025} and manually correcting them structure by structure.
For the validation portion of the subset, labels were produced from scratch, contoured independently without reference to any automated prediction, so that the validation labels are not anchored to the adult-trained model.
