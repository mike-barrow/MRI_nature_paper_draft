\subsection*{Expert image-quality assessment}\label{subsec:imgqc}
Each examination underwent a scan-by-scan image-quality assessment by [TBD -> Heike: a board-certified radiologist], who rated diagnostic image quality on a 5-point Likert scale ranging from 1 (non-diagnostic) to 5 (perfect quality), as defined in Table~\ref{tab:qc}.
The purpose of this screening is to identify examinations whose image quality is too low for reliable annotation, rather than to confirm that the segmentations are correct.

\begin{table}[h]
\caption{Image-quality scoring scale used by the expert reviewer.}\label{tab:qc}
\begin{tabular}{@{}cl@{}}
\toprule
Score & Description \\
\midrule
5 & Perfect scan, no artifacts. \\
4 & Mild artifacts, fully diagnostic. \\
3 & Mild to moderate artifacts, repeat if an abnormality were noticed. \\
2 & Substantial artifacts, large abnormalities discernible but repeat needed. \\
1 & Completely non-diagnostic. \\
\botrule
\end{tabular}

The data additionally pass the curation and validation performed by the host repository as part of submission.
