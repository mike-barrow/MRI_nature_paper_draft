\abstract{Image-based artificial intelligence tools increasingly support clinical imaging,
yet whole-body datasets that pair anatomical and functional information remain limited to adults
and dominated by computed tomography.
This restricts pediatric use, because anatomy changes across development and ionising radiation is avoided in children,
so models calibrated on adult data behave unpredictably on pediatric inputs.
Lymphoma staging and treatment monitoring depend on whole-body positron emission tomography combined with magnetic resonance imaging,
where anatomical MRI localises disease and functional PET quantifies metabolic activity,
but no pediatric PET/MRI dataset with lesion and treatment-response labels exists.
We present a pediatric whole-body PET/MRI dataset of 250 examinations from 134 patients, spanning ages 1 to 25 years.
It includes disease-free and pathological examinations with anatomy segmentations, lesion annotations, and staging and metabolic-response labels.
We describe the acquisition, organisation, and validation of the dataset,
and its potential to support development and benchmarking of pediatric whole-body anatomical and oncological analysis tools.}
