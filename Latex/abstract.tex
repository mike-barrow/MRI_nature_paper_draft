\abstract{Image-based artificial intelligence increasingly supports clinical imaging,
yet whole-body datasets that pair anatomical and functional information remain largely adult and dominated by computed tomography.
This limits pediatric use, since anatomy changes across development and computed tomography is avoided in children to limit radiation exposure,
so adult-trained models transfer poorly.
Whole-body positron emission tomography combined with magnetic resonance imaging (PET/MRI) is well suited to pediatric oncology,
where MRI localizes disease and PET quantifies metabolic activity.
We present a pediatric whole-body PET/MRI dataset of [TBD] examinations from [TBD] patients, aged [TBD] years,
comprising examinations with active lymphoma and disease-free follow-up examinations.
It provides multi-structure anatomical and tumor-lesion segmentations.
We describe the acquisition, organization, and validation of the dataset,
and its use for developing and benchmarking pediatric whole-body anatomical and oncological analysis tools.}
