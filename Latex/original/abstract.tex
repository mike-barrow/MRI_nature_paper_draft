\abstract{Whole-body PET/MRI is well established in pediatric oncology,
yet the public datasets that drive medical image analysis are almost entirely adult and CT-based,
and models built on them generalize poorly to children.
We present a pediatric whole-body PET/MRI dataset of [TBD] examinations from [TBD] patients (ages [TBD] years).
Each examination pairs anatomical MRI with functional FDG-PET,
and the release provides multi-structure anatomical segmentations spanning the skeleton and major organs, tumor-lesion segmentations, and disease-free follow-up examinations as pathology-negative references.
We describe the cohort, acquisition, organization, and technical validation,
and outline its use for pediatric whole-body anatomical segmentation, PET-guided lesion detection, and adult-to-pediatric transfer learning.}
